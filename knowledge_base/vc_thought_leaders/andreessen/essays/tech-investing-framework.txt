## The a16z Investment Philosophy

"We want to invest in the future. We're not interested in incremental improvements - we want companies that are building something fundamentally new."

### Core Principles

1. **Software is eating the world** - Every industry will be transformed
2. **Bet on people** - Great founders can pivot and adapt
3. **Network effects matter** - They create sustainable moats
4. **Timing is everything** - Right idea, wrong time = failure
5. **Full-stack approach** - Help companies with more than just capital

## Evaluating Technology Companies

### The Product Question

"Is this 10x better than existing solutions, or just 10% better?"

**10x improvements win:**
- Users will switch for dramatic improvements
- Word-of-mouth drives organic growth
- Pricing power emerges naturally

**10% improvements struggle:**
- Switching costs exceed benefits
- Requires outspending incumbents on marketing
- Easily replicated by competition

### The Market Question

"Is this a large market, or can it become one?"

**Market size considerations:**
- Current market size (bottom-up TAM)
- Market expansion potential (new use cases)
- Market creation possibility (new behavior)
- Adjacent market opportunities

"We'd rather invest in a small team addressing a huge market than a large team addressing a small market."

### The Team Question

"Would I work for this founder?"

**What we look for:**
- Technical excellence and domain expertise
- Missionary vs. mercenary motivation
- Ability to recruit A+ talent
- Coachability combined with conviction
- Communication skills (for fundraising, hiring, selling)

### The Timing Question

"Why now? What has changed to make this possible today that wasn't possible before?"

**Valid "why now" answers:**
- Technology breakthrough (AI, mobile, cloud)
- Regulatory change
- Behavior shift (pandemic accelerated digital)
- Cost curve crossing threshold
- Infrastructure maturity

## Business Model Patterns

### SaaS (Software as a Service)

**Attractive characteristics:**
- Recurring revenue
- High gross margins (70-80%+)
- Negative churn possible (expansion > contraction)
- Predictable growth

**Key metrics:**
- ARR growth rate
- Net revenue retention
- CAC payback period
- Gross margin

### Marketplace

**Attractive characteristics:**
- Network effects between supply and demand
- High take rates possible once established
- Winner-take-all dynamics

**Key metrics:**
- GMV growth
- Take rate trends
- Supply/demand liquidity
- Repeat purchase rates

### Consumer

**Attractive characteristics:**
- Viral growth potential
- Global scale possible rapidly
- Strong brand value

**Key metrics:**
- DAU/MAU ratio
- Viral coefficient
- Retention curves
- Engagement depth

## Network Effects Deep Dive

"Network effects are the closest thing to a sustainable competitive advantage in technology."

### Types of Network Effects

**1. Direct Network Effects**
- More users → more valuable for each user
- Example: Social networks, messaging

**2. Indirect Network Effects**
- More of one side → attracts more of other side
- Example: Marketplaces, platforms

**3. Data Network Effects**
- More users → more data → better product
- Example: AI/ML companies, search

**4. Ecosystem Network Effects**
- More developers/partners → more value
- Example: App stores, cloud platforms

### Measuring Network Effect Strength

- Organic growth percentage
- CAC trends over time
- Retention improvement with usage
- Pricing power indicators
- Competitive switching rates

## The Platform Playbook

"The most valuable companies are platforms, not products."

### Platform Characteristics

1. **Multi-sided:** Connects different user types
2. **Extensible:** Others can build on top
3. **Defensible:** Gets stronger over time
4. **Scalable:** Technology enables massive scale

### Platform Evolution

**Stage 1:** Single killer app (attract users)
**Stage 2:** Open APIs (attract developers)
**Stage 3:** Ecosystem (third parties create value)
**Stage 4:** Platform lock-in (switching costs high)

## Red Flags

### Business Red Flags

- Depending on paid acquisition for all growth
- Declining unit economics at scale
- No clear path to profitability
- Over-reliance on single customer/channel
- Technology that's easily replicable

### Team Red Flags

- Founders not getting along
- Key person dependencies
- Inability to recruit top talent
- Mercenary motivation
- Unwillingness to adapt

### Market Red Flags

- Timing too early (infrastructure not ready)
- Timing too late (incumbents have adapted)
- Regulatory uncertainty that's fundamental
- Customer willingness to pay unclear
- No clear "why now"
