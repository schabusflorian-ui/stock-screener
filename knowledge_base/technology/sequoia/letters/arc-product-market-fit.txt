## Product-Market Fit

"The number one cause of startup failure is making something nobody wants."

### Signs of Product-Market Fit

**Strong signals:**
- Organic growth without marketing spend
- Users actively recommending to others
- Usage increasing over time per user
- High retention rates
- Customers willing to pay more

**Weak signals (don't be fooled):**
- Growth from paid acquisition only
- Users trying once and leaving
- Heavy discounting required
- Lots of feature requests but low usage
- Growth that depends on founder effort

### The PMF Pyramid

**Level 1: Problem-Solution Fit**
- You've identified a real problem
- People acknowledge the pain point
- They're actively seeking solutions

**Level 2: Product-Market Fit**
- Your solution works for the problem
- Users are adopting and staying
- Word-of-mouth is happening

**Level 3: Channel-Market Fit**
- You've found scalable distribution
- Unit economics work at scale
- Growth is predictable

## Building a Legendary Company

### The Company Building Framework

**1. People**
"A company is simply a group of people organized to achieve something together."
- Hire people smarter than yourself
- Culture is what people do when you're not watching
- A-players attract A-players; B-players hire C-players

**2. Market**
"Big markets make big companies."
- Better to be in a rising tide
- Can you expand the market?
- Is the timing right?

**3. Product**
"Make something people want."
- Start with the customer problem
- Build the minimum to learn
- Iterate based on usage data

**4. Business Model**
"How do you capture value?"
- Unit economics must work
- Path to profitability clear
- Sustainable competitive advantage

## Founder Qualities

### What Separates the Best

**1. Clarity of Vision**
"The best founders can describe their company in one sentence, and that sentence is compelling."
- Not just what they're building
- Why it matters
- How it changes the world

**2. Intensity of Execution**
"Ideas are cheap. Execution is everything."
- Speed as a competitive advantage
- High standards without perfectionism
- Relentless focus on what matters

**3. Ability to Recruit**
"Your ability to recruit is the best predictor of success."
- Can you sell the vision?
- Do people want to work with you?
- Can you attract people better than yourself?

**4. Resilience**
"Startups are a marathon of sprints."
- Setbacks are inevitable
- How you respond matters more
- Persistence without stubbornness

## Growth Framework

### Sustainable vs. Unsustainable Growth

**Sustainable growth comes from:**
- Product excellence driving word-of-mouth
- Efficient paid acquisition (CAC < LTV)
- Network effects compounding
- Expansion revenue from existing customers

**Unsustainable growth comes from:**
- Buying growth at any cost
- Discounting to drive volume
- Paying for retention
- Press/hype without substance

### The Growth Equation

Sustainable Growth = Acquisition × Retention × Expansion

- **Acquisition:** How many new customers?
- **Retention:** How many stay?
- **Expansion:** Do they buy more over time?

All three must be healthy for sustainable growth.

## Capital Efficiency

"Great companies are built by capital efficiency, not capital abundance."

### Principles

1. **Extend runway:** More time = more chances to iterate
2. **Focus resources:** Do fewer things better
3. **Earn the right to scale:** Prove it works before scaling
4. **Build real value:** Revenue > funding as validation

### Warning Signs of Capital Inefficiency

- Hiring ahead of need
- Marketing spend without unit economics
- Building features nobody uses
- Over-investing in infrastructure
- Spending to hit vanity metrics

## Long-Term Orientation

"We measure success in decades, not quarters."

### Building for Durability

1. **Customer obsession:** They determine your success
2. **Mission over money:** Purpose attracts talent and customers
3. **Culture as moat:** Hard to replicate
4. **Continuous improvement:** Never stop getting better
5. **Long-term thinking:** Invest in the future

### The Sequoia Test

"If this company went away, would customers notice? Would they care? Would they fight to bring it back?"

Great companies answer yes to all three.
