## The Central Bank Framework

Stanley Druckenmiller's investment philosophy centers on one key insight: central bank policy, particularly the Federal Reserve, is the dominant driver of asset prices. Understanding this framework is essential for macro investing.

### Core Principle: Follow the Liquidity

"Don't fight the Fed" isn't just a cliche - it's the foundation of macro investing. When the Fed is injecting liquidity into the system, risk assets tend to rise. When the Fed is withdrawing liquidity, risk assets tend to fall.

Key liquidity indicators to watch:
1. Fed balance sheet expansion/contraction
2. M2 money supply growth
3. Reverse repo facility usage
4. Bank reserves
5. Credit spreads (indicator of financial conditions)

### The Fed Pivot Trade

The most profitable trades often come at Fed policy inflection points:

**Dovish Pivot Signals:**
- Slowing pace of rate hikes
- Increased emphasis on employment mandate
- Language softening on inflation
- Financial stability concerns emerging
- Action: Get long risk assets before the market fully prices it in

**Hawkish Pivot Signals:**
- Acceleration of rate hikes
- Hawkish Fed speakers
- Inflation remaining elevated
- Strong employment data
- Action: Reduce risk, potentially go short

### Timing the Cycle

"Never invest in the present. The present is already priced in."

Druckenmiller looks 18-24 months ahead:
1. Where will inflation be?
2. Where will the economy be in the cycle?
3. What will the Fed be doing?
4. What does the market expect vs. what's likely?

The edge comes from correctly anticipating divergence between market expectations and reality.

### Position Sizing Based on Fed Regime

**High Conviction Fed Tailwind:**
- Balance sheet expansion + rate cuts
- Maximum long exposure
- Use leverage on conviction ideas
- "Go for the jugular"

**Fed Headwind:**
- Balance sheet contraction + rate hikes
- Reduce exposure significantly
- Focus on capital preservation
- Wait for better opportunities

**Transitional Periods:**
- Most dangerous times
- Reduce position sizes
- Wait for clarity
- Watch for policy mistakes

### Reading the Fed's Body Language

Beyond the official statements, watch for:
- Dissenting votes and their direction
- Changes in dot plot clustering
- Unusual emphasis in speeches
- Policy mistakes they're trying to correct
- External pressures (fiscal policy, global events)

### Historical Patterns

Major market tops often coincide with:
- Peak Fed hawkishness
- Maximum rate hike expectations
- Financial conditions at tightest levels

Major market bottoms often coincide with:
- Fed pivot to dovish
- Expectations of rate cuts
- Financial conditions easing

### The Druckenmiller Test

Before any major position, ask:
1. What is the Fed doing now?
2. What will the Fed be doing in 18-24 months?
3. Is this already priced in?
4. If I'm wrong, how much can I lose?
5. If I'm right, how much can I make?

Only take the trade if the risk/reward is asymmetric in your favor.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. Fighting the Fed's direction
2. Trading on current data instead of forward expectations
3. Over-diversifying during high-conviction opportunities
4. Under-sizing positions when you have edge
5. Staying too long after the regime changes
6. Confusing volatility with opportunity
