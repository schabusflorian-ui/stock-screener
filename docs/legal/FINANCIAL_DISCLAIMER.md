## Investment Risks

### General Risks
All investments involve risk, including but not limited to:

- **Market Risk**: Investments can lose value due to market conditions
- **Volatility**: Prices can fluctuate significantly and rapidly
- **Liquidity Risk**: You may not be able to sell when you want
- **Currency Risk**: International investments have exchange rate risk
- **Interest Rate Risk**: Changes in interest rates affect security values
- **Credit Risk**: Issuers may default on obligations
- **Political Risk**: Government actions can affect investments
- **Total Loss**: You can lose your entire investment

### Specific Risks

**Equities**: Stock prices can be volatile. Company-specific events can cause significant price changes.

**IPOs**: Initial public offerings are especially risky. Limited historical data is available.

**International Securities**: Additional risks include currency fluctuations, different regulatory environments, and geopolitical events.

**Small-Cap and Micro-Cap Stocks**: Higher volatility and liquidity risk compared to large-cap stocks.

**Options and Derivatives**: Complex instruments with high risk of loss.

**Past performance is not indicative of future results.**
